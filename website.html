Autumn

It's okay to stay inside the cocoon of your emotions,
And let silence take charge.
It's okay to have nothing to look forward to,
And nothing to look behind.
It's okay to feel cold and numb,
And embrace darkness and not light
It's okay not to feel the ecstasy in the breeze,
And keep feelings inside memoirs.
There are countless adjectives for colours but,
Who loves the shades of grey?
Who loves autumn after it's yellow fades away?

Hope

I agree I've implored the stars to illuminate hope inside me,
I've always asked Moon, "When will this darkness end?"
I've wandered the streets in black nights, collecting her silvery shade.
But now I see, hope's a murder of seed that could have bloomed into beautiful beginnings,
Hope delays the final collapse, keeping you pinned to despair,
Like a wingless bird, clinging for flight, a crushed insect fighting to breathe.
It sucks out life drop by drop, until the last trickle of belief wipes out,
killing you in ways much painful than despair.
If ever there's hope, but not enough to count on, it shouldn't cross the path of a life, fighting its way out of the cocoon.
It shouldn't ever come at all.

Shadow

I always try to outrun my shadow,
The demon of my past sits there.
A tiny speck of light and he follows me,
So I find solace only at dusk.
The moon peeks through my window sill,
And caress my scars with her silvery light.
The stars carry me to the dreamland,
Where the wind sings me a lullaby.
And then I blink my eyes off from the lovely old dream,
The dawn arrives with the fiend,
And misery resurges with the rising sun,
Revealing the pieces of my broken soul.
And the demon follows me.
Always behind me.

What it is to paint you!

They say I'm a good painter;
I know I'm not, I can never paint you;
What if I paint those depths in your doe-brown eyes?
And get myself drowned in the ocean of love;
Can I ever fathom a hue to paint your lips with?
Those curves which can keep me afloat in a raging sea;
How am I supposed to brush that hair on your cheeks?
Which when rubbed against mine wipes me off all the pain;
Am I expected to paint those jet- black curls?
The enchanting waves in which I lose myself.
I never knew what magic is until I saw you;
If they want to know what it looks like,
They can see you in my eyes.

What the stars say?

Would you mind coming along to a quiet place in the moor, when the breeze sing serenades, on one of those nights when the moon is full? Will you listen to the chaos of the crickets till the wind lulls them to sleep? Will, you then listen to the outburst of my mind, my emotions flickering as those fireflies around? Will you listen to me till the last trickle of the misery filling me drains out, till the whirlpool of this deep ocean dies out, till I'm completely empty?

It's not that I love speaking, but when silence fills in, I long for someone who could listen to me just as those stars listen to me every night. Don't worry. You wouldn't feel as those tiny little stars feel. I would listen to you too but, will you then hold me in your embrace and listen with me the harmony of the stars above?

You'd wonder what those stars have to say. They would tell you how much I love you and would ask you to love me back.

Self-love

Every autumn trees lose their leaves, stand high and exultant throughout the winter, flaunting their bared body. They wait for the spring to come, and when it arrives, they bloom once again. I never knew how to say - NO, a two lettered word quite simple to spell, but hard to use. People came and left just like winter snow taking away pieces of me, stripping my soul bit by bit. I gave everything till all of it was gone.

Whenever I looked into the mirror, I could only see a puzzle with so many missing pieces, an edifice crumbled under the freight of heavy breaths. I found that sneers stuck to me quicker than any applause. For some reason, I always grappled in accepting compliments. I believed that anyone who says you look beautiful, I love your smile, you are genius, you'll do great, and anything pleasant to me was being cynical or maybe because they only want to cheer me up. Instead of a simple 'Thank you,' I always felt an urge to ask, 'Why.'

But things were very different when someone said you are arrogant, you are stingy or you are cold-hearted. I would pin all those remarks to the deepest chambers in my heart. Yes, I felt miserable seeing people think so little of me, but thoughts of them being untruthful or repugnant would never traverse my mind.

But now I apprehend, happiness is like a sand in a closed fist. The harder you try to clasp onto it, the more it spills out. It's futile to solicit happiness in the perceptions of people. Until you learn to love yourself, not anything in the world can help you. People will come, and they'll leave, but what stays behind are tiny little pieces of you, one less each time. It is you who has to tow yourself in for rehabilitation. Nobody else will. They'd only demand one more piece from your dilapidated soul.

So, this is the time for you to groom yourself. Today is for you to bloom; Winter's gone, and Spring's at your door. Who else are you waiting for?
